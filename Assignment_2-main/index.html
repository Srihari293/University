Happiness comparison around the world (2016-2019)

Introduction

The United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network publishes a yearly report on the current state of happiness in the world. The report analyzes countries worldwide based on metrics like income, healthy life expectancy, generosity, freedom, trust, and social support in the final tally. My goal is to analyze the trends and find correlations between the factors that cause countries to have higher happiness scores. 😁

People make people happy

We can all agree that people make people happy. Having the company of friends and family contributes to happiness. We can see that countries with higher life expectancy tend to have higher happiness scores. But why do some countries have a poor life expectancy? There could be several factors, but one standout factor is the per capita GDP of the country.

If the country has less GDP, it means that the quality and quantity of goods and services are reduced. Not only does this mean that the standard of living is lower, but the price of what is considered premium services in well off countries like proper health care is also higher due to scarcity. The bubble chart depicts GDP, life expectancy, and happiness.

Generosity and happiness


From the bubble chart above we see a clear trend. As the GDP increases, the life expectancy starts to increase. That is, as purchasing power of people and the availability of goods and services increases, people are more likely to lead a happy and long life. In this section we will look at how generosity, gdp and happiness are related.

From the bar chart we can infer that generosity is not very much related to GDP, but does improve the average happiness score. The fact that it is not related to GDP tells us that sharing resources and helping others is a practice and varies from region to region. We can see that despite having a low happiness score, the Southeastern region of Asia is more generous than the rest of the world.

This bar chart visualization suggests a positve trend between generosity and happniess score.

Trust on the government

One of the main factors that affect the happiness of a country is how it is run. If the people in power are tyrannical and abuse their power, the state of the country will be very poor. For instance, over 180 million unhappy government workers protested in India on September 2nd 2016, demanding increase in minimum wage.

President Dilma Rousseff of Brazil and president Park Geun-hye of South Korea were impeached resulting in a better happiness score. However, the score in the US and UK dropped when Donald Trump was elected president and Britain voted to leave the European Union.
